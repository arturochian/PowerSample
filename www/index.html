Power and Sample Size with spower from Hmisc

This example demonstrates the flexibility of spower and related functions from Hmisc. We simulate a 2-arm (350 subjects/arm) 5-year follow-up study for wich the control group's survival distribution is Weibull with 1-year survival of .95 and 3-year survival of .7. All subjects are followed at least one year, and patients enter the study with linearly increasing probability starting with zero. Assume (1) there is no chance of dropin for the first 6 months, then the probability increases linearly up to .15 at 5 years; (2) there is a linearly increasing chance of dropout up to .3 at 5 years; and (3) the treatment has no effect for the first 9 months, then it has a constant effect (hazard ratio of .75).
